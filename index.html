Research Interests

I am interested in integrating neuroimaging methods in novel ways to identify early risk markers and mechanisms underlying psychopathology. Identifying early risk factors is critical for understanding the pathophysiology of an illness because it helps to dissociate mechanisms involved in the initial onset of an illness from markers that are a consequence or ‘scar’ of an illness. My past research models both structural (how regions physically connect) and functional (how regions communicate) connectivity in the cortico-striatal network to predicts clinical symptoms and risk behaviors, including risk taking and reward sensitivity. I am currently building on these connectivity models to investigate how early markers intersect with cognitive development and decision making while exploring graph theoretical approaches and simultaneous EEG-MRI analyses.
