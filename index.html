Research Interests

I am interested in integrating neuroimaging methods in novel ways to identify early risk markers and mechanisms underlying psychopathology. My past research models both structural (how regions physically connect) and functional (how regions communicate) connectivity in networks to predict clinical symptoms and risk behaviors. I am currently building on these connectivity models to investigate how early markers intersect with cognitive development and decision making while exploring graph theoretical approaches and simultaneous EEG-MRI analyses.

Katherine SF Damme

PhD

My research identifies neural corollaries of severe psychopathology (including bipolar spectrum disorder and psychosis) that may be therapeutic targets to mitigating risk for initial illness onset or symptom progression. My previous work involved identifying corollaries of risk included stable structural brain features, structural connectivity and functional neural networks. In a parallel line of work, I examined the complex behavioral and cognitive features of executive function. During my post-doctoral fellowship, I am building on my previous research (identifying therapeutic targets to ameliorate risk) by examining the therapeutic impact of exercise and tDCS on mechanisms underlying risk for psychopathology.
